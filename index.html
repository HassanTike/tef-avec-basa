TEF Canada

Official French proficiency test recognized by IRCC for immigration, studies, and work in Canada.

IRCC Recognition

Accepted for Express Entry, study permits, and permanent residency applications.

Exam Structure

Four sections: Reading, Listening, Speaking, Writing. Computer- or paper-based depending on center.

Scoring & Levels

Results mapped to CLB/NCLC levels; higher bands strengthen your profile.

Registration & Dates

Flexible sessions year-round. Get guidance on nearest centers and required documents.

Preparation & Coaching

Diagnostic tests, targeted lessons, mock exams with feedback to boost your score.

Exam Sections

Reading

Multiple-choice tasks assessing comprehension of articles, notices, and formal texts. Manage time, scan for keywords, and infer meaning from context.

Listening

Audio extracts from everyday and professional situations. Focus on main ideas, speakers’ intent, and details such as dates and figures.

Speaking

Two tasks: interaction and expression. Structure your ideas, use connectors, and demonstrate clarity, accuracy, and spontaneity.

Writing

Two tasks: functional writing and argumentation. Plan, write clearly, and revise to ensure coherence, register, and grammar.
